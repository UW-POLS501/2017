Finding Data

Jeffrey Arnold

1/9/2017

Here are links to some datasets potentially of interest to political scientists. However, the data collection process in academia is disaggregated, with many data sets collected by researchers for specific purposes and distributed by the researcher with their paper.

See also the Washington Library Data Research Guide

Some R packages

  • ROpenGov has R packages to access various government related data.
  • For packages to access data on the web from within R see the Open Data Task View
  • wbstats World Bank statistics
  • psData: Get some common political science data sets
  • countrycode: Correspondence between common country codes: e.g. COW, World Bank, ISO.

Repositories

  • Harvard Dataverse Contains replication data for many political science articles (among other data). The R package dvn allows for direct download and upload of data to and from the Dataverse.
  • ICPSR Collection of many political science and economics datasets.
  • figshare
  • Open Science Framework
  • Zenodo

Economics

  • Quandl A collection of financial and economic data. The R package Quandl allows for easy access to Quandl data within R.
  • World Development Indicators (WDI). The R package WDI allows for downloading data.
  • Penn World Table
  • UNCTAD Trade and FDI data
  • WTO Trade and Tariff data
  • FRED Collection of U.S. economic data by the Federal Reserve Bank of St. Louis.
